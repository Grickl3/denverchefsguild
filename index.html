Welcome to the

Denver Chefs Guild

The Denver Chefs Guild is a volunteer-powered, not-for-profit organization which serves the chefs and food workers in the Denver metro area. We strive to create a better world for food workers through food justice. We believe in collaboration and camaraderie. Join us now and strengthen our mission and vision for a better world.

Our Purpose

We are devoted to creating a space that strengthens our community, facilitates connection, and supports the health of Denver’s food workers. As we embrace the philosophy of “think globally, act locally”, we support food workers from around the world who are committed to creating a socially just and equitable world within our local community. We strive to create a nourishing and nurturing environment for our food workers that fosters a greater sense of belonging, mindfulness, and growth. Even though we are based in Denver, we hope to inspire and be inspired by the wonderful individuals and organizations around the world who are working toward similar objectives.

Why join us?

Currently there are few organizations and networks which value every single food worker regardless of their jobs and inclusively support them in the industry. There is elitism, hierarchy, and ego that impacts the lives and health of all food workers. We are here to serve our community in full transparency. We respect privacy and confidentiality and we are committed to serving everyone — regardless of their positions or status. Whether you are a porter, restaurateur, prep-cook, dishwasher, line cook, or chef the Denver Chefs Guild is open to you.

What We Offer Our Members

As a member, you will be a part of a vibrant local digital space which consistently enriches, and supports our local culinary community. Through our electronic newsletter and subscription-based emails you will receive the most up-to-date information on resources and relevant programs that impact your everyday life. As we continue creating space for individuals, employers, and organizations to support the culinary community, we will continue developing:

  • A crowd-sourced database, facilitating connection between Denver-area chefs, employers, and organizations
  • Online resource libraries to address the most pressing needs of chefs and food workers
  • Methods for fostering a growth mindset in our changing industry
  • Efforts to cultivate imagination, innovation, and transformation of kitchen work culture through community
  • A platform to publish and discover stories of food workers, chefs, and restaurateurs from different walks of life

How You Can Support Us

Your financial contribution will go towards providing support services to the food workers who need it most. You can support the mission in a number of ways:

  • Become a member!
  • Spread the word — Let other food workers know that they have support.
  • Sponsor members — If you know a chef or food worker in the Denver area who would like to be involved or who would benefit from our efforts, please give them a gift of a membership of Denver Chefs Guild.
  • Donate — We are currently engaged in building digital resources and tools to provide comprehensive services to our food workers. We are looking for individual contributions and sponsors who would help us pay for the technical expenses.
  • Fulfill our wishlist: We will be posting our wishlist on the website. All contributions are appreciated.

We know that things are difficult in these challenging times, so no amount is too small for a good cause. We have enormous appreciation and deep gratitude for your generosity and willingness to contribute to this important work.

About US

The Denver Chefs Guild stands before you as a coalition of like-minded individuals from around the world, from different walks of life. What binds them together is not their work as chefs but their commitment to the issues surrounding the health and wellness of those who maintain the industry in order to provide healthy, sumptuous food to those whom they serve.

Five years ago, Anders Benson, a graduate from the Culinary Institute of America, never imagined that he would be starting an organization like this guild. Yet, he felt a deep stirring inside him — a restlessness — that made him thoughtful about the meaning and purpose of the oppressive work environment in the food industry. In 2014, Anders joined Denver Montessori Junior/Senior High School as the Culinary Science Teacher. Through the concepts of physics, chemistry, and biology he unfolded a world of Culinary Arts to his students; he also took classes in permaculture design as well as earned his MA in Education. Through his readings, musings, and interactions with the students, he was inspired to reflect upon the life and world of chefs, and became increasingly uneasy about the lives of the chefs — those who had committed their lives to feeding the mind and body of the community, but were getting depleted of their own strength through physical and emotional burnout. Anders began to question the food that is served on the table — food which is not only a labor of love but the labor of sweat, exploitation, pain, and stress. Anders decided to challenge these systems. He initiated conversations on various issues and ideas connected with the food industry, eventually motivating him to develop a forum for social action.
